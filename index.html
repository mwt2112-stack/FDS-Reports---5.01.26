How to Use FDS
Start with the question → find the category → pick the summary report → drill to detail only when needed.
01
Category 01
Budget & Financial Review
Budget vs. actuals, available balances, and project performance
Category 01

Budget & Financial Review

Best used when: You need to know how you're doing overall — budget, spending, balance, and performance across a project or department.
Best Starting Points
  • 📄 Project Summary Statement — single project, budget vs actual
  • 📋 Department Summary Statement — roll-up across all dept projects
    Shows all projects under a department node with budget, actuals, and available balance rolled up. Best used for departmental financial health review. Parameters: Business Unit, Department/Rollup, Fiscal Year.
  • 📄 SPFR – Summary by Budget Category — sponsored project performance
Use Cases
  • See spending across all projects in a department
  • What's the budget for this project?
  • How much spent this year vs. last year?
  • Are certain categories spending faster?
  • What's our revenue vs. expense position?
The Strongest Starting Point
The Project Summary Statement is the best overall starting point for financial health and high-level performance review. Always start here before drilling into detail reports.
Budget vs Actual Balance Tracking Project Performance

Category 02
Payroll Reports
Salary charges, allocations, fringe, and labor activity
Category 02

Payroll Reports

Best used when: You need to review salary charges, payroll allocations, fringe, or employee-level labor activity.
📄 Payroll Detail Report
  • Employee First & Last Name
  • Combo Code & Earn Code
  • Pay Periods, Salary & Fringe
  • Cost Transfer ID (if applicable)
Does NOT show: Encumbrances, Project-to-Date Totals
📄 Payroll Summary Report
  • Employee Name & Combo Code
  • Current Period, FY-to-Date, Project-to-Date Salary
  • Payroll Encumbrances
Does NOT show: Earn Code, Period Dates, Cost Transfer ID
Formula
Payroll = Salary (earn code × combo code) + Fringe (rate × salary). Payroll posts in two pieces — salary and fringe are separate.
Salary Monitoring Effort Certification Payroll Encumbrances

Category 03
Sponsored Project Management
Grant and contract spending, award balances, and receivables
Category 03

Sponsored Project Management

Best used when: You need to monitor grant and contract spending, award balances, budget categories, receivables, or sponsor-related financial activity.
Best Starting Points
  • 📄 SPFR – Summary by Budget Category — award health by budget category, actuals & encumbrances
  • 📋 Sponsored Project Financial Report – Detail — transaction-level view of award charges
    Transaction-level view of all charges against a sponsored award. Shows individual vouchers, journals, and payroll entries by account. Use when the summary raises a question about a specific charge. Parameters: Project ID, Activity, Accounting Period.
  • 📋 SAPO Budget vs Encumbrance — subaward commitments
    Shows the budget, encumbrance, and remaining balance for each SAPO (Sponsored Award Purchase Order). Use to confirm subaward spending is correctly encumbered and within the awarded amount. Critical for grants with subcontracts.
Use Cases
  • How much funding remains on the award?
  • Are there unusual charges on the project?
  • What is the project burn rate?
  • Are there receivables or billing issues?
  • Is the F&A allocation correct?
Formula
Sponsored Available = Award Budget − Direct Costs − F&A − Encumbrances. Always check at the budget category level — not just the project total.
Project Performance Effort Certification Annual Reporting Compliance

Category 04
Transaction & Operational Reporting
Day-to-day transactions, reconciliation, and detail review
Category 04

Transaction & Operational Reporting

Best used when: You need to see day-to-day financial transactions, validate what exactly posted, and reconcile detailed transaction activity.
Best Starting Points
  • 📄 Trial Balance by Account Detail — what posted this period
  • 📄 Payroll Detail Report — salary transaction-level view
  • 📋 P-Card Drillback — P-Card charges and merchant detail
    Shows P-Card holder, card number, merchant (vendor), transaction amount, date, approver, ChartString, and budget header status. Use for reviewing P-Card activity and verifying charges posted correctly. Does NOT show monthly card limits.
Use Cases
  • What exactly posted during the period?
  • Which transactions hit this ChartString?
  • Do the transactions reconcile?
  • Which charges need review or correction?
  • What to use for monthly transaction review?
Why It Matters
Essential for validating the underlying activity behind balances, catching coding errors early, and ensuring the books support what users expect to see.
Monthly Close Prep Weekly Reconciliation

Category 05
Procurement Reports
Purchase orders, open commitments, and P-Card activity
Category 05

Procurement Reports

Best used when: You need to review purchasing activity, open commitments, P-Card charges, encumbrances, or voucher payments.
📄 Purchase Order Open Commitment
  • PO Number & Vendor
  • ChartString & Total PO Amount
  • Cumulative Expensed & Open Vouchers
  • Remaining PO Balance (SAPOs & Non-SAPOs)
📋 P-Card Drillback
Shows P-Card holder, card number, merchant, amount, transaction date, approver, ChartString, and budget header status. Use to review P-Card activity, verify charges, and confirm allocations. Does NOT show monthly card limits.
  • P-Card Holder, Number & Merchant
  • Amount & Transaction Date
  • P-Card Approver & ChartString
  • Budget Header Status & Comments
Key Insight
Open commitments are often the hidden reason available balances appear lower than expected. Always check PO commitments before concluding a budget has overspent.
Monthly Review Quarterly Fiscal Year-End

Category 06
Closing the Fiscal Year
Year-end review, accruals, overdrafts, and cleanup
Category 06

Closing the Fiscal Year

Best used when: You need to review outstanding items before year-end close — accruals, overdrafts, PO rollovers, and cleanup.
Best Starting Points
  • 📄 Purchase Order Open Commitment
  • 📋 Fund Balance – Overdraft Reports
    Identifies accounts in deficit (overdrawn) position. At year-end, deficits must be resolved before close. Review and correct any negative balances with your SBO before June 30th.
  • 📄 Project Summary Statement — overall project balance
  • 📄 Trial Balance by Account Detail — transaction-level cleanup
Additional Reports
  • Financial Statements
  • Fiscal Year-End Encumbrance Report
  • Fiscal Year-End Closed POs
  • Accrued P-Cards & Vouchers
Timeline — Late April through June
Year-end is a distinct control point. This group ensures outstanding items are treated correctly, accruals are complete, and the close process is supported with clean, defensible reporting.
Late April — June Fiscal Year-End
